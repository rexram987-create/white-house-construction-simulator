# White House Construction Simulator

An interactive historical website about the construction, burning, restoration, and expansion of the White House.

## Project Goal

The goal is to build an educational digital museum that begins as an interactive timeline and later grows into a 3D construction simulator.

The first version includes:

- A dark museum-style homepage
- An interactive timeline
- Historical information cards
- A placeholder for a future 3D reconstruction
- A bibliography file for sources

## Historical Scope

The project follows the White House through major stages:

1. 1791–1792 — choosing the site and planning the President's House
2. 1792 — cornerstone and beginning of construction
3. 1793–1797 — walls, stonework, and main structure
4. 1798–1800 — interior work and first occupancy by John Adams
5. 1814 — Burning of Washington
6. 1815–1817 — restoration after the fire
7. 1902 — Theodore Roosevelt and the West Wing
8. 1948–1952 — Truman Reconstruction

## Planned Future Features

- Three.js 3D model viewer
- Layer-by-layer building animation
- 3D stages: foundations, walls, roof, windows, fire damage, and restoration
- Historical image gallery
- Public-domain portraits and maps
- Modal image viewer
- Detailed source citations
